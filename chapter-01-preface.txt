The following book is the latest version of materials that I use in my remote coaching module on Race Course Decision Making. The online content is in a continual state of improvement and updates, in part because I am constantly trying to improve the curriculum, but to a large extent because these ideas evolve each year that I coach. As such, in some sections you might find references to materials that are not yet available. Despite this, the aim is for each section to be able to stand on its own with descriptions and figures in this book to explain the concepts in a thorough way.

The objective of writing this book has been as much an exercise for me to organize my own thoughts, as it has been a mission to explain many of the concepts that I teach to sailors and teams that I coach. It is in an ongoing process of revision as I learn more and adapt these ideas and techniques, but if I don't publish it in some way now, it may sit in my personal archive forever, as I will probably never really consider it to be "complete"!

More important than the specific ideas archived here, are the thought process behind them. To become one of the top sailors in the world, it is not enough to memorize facts or follow directions on technique; top teams are thought leaders and pioneers, who study extensively to learn what has come before, and then strive to push the bar forward. This process can be creative at times, while scientific and methodical at others. It is almost certainly messy, and it is far from a straight line. Hopefully the ideas in this book - and more importantly, the thought process behind them - help you to look at your race course decision making in a new and interesting way, and to take a journey down your own path of learning that produces results on the race course!
